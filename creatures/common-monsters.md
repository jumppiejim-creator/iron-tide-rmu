# Common Monsters  Combat Reference

## Skeletons

**Source:** Creature Law, Undead section. Look up specific stat blocks when needed.

General guidelines for skeleton encounters:
- AT 3-4 (bone provides some protection)
- No bleeding (undead don't bleed)
- Immune to Puncture criticals (no organs to puncture)  treat as Krush
- Fear-causing: characters must make Fear RR when first encountering undead
- Vulnerable to Krush criticals (bones shatter)
- Typically wield weapons from life

## Wolves

**Source:** Creature Law, Dogs section (wolves are large dogs).

Use the Dog, Medium stat block with adjustments for wolves:
- Typically slightly larger and more aggressive than domesticated dogs
- Pack tactics: multiple wolves flanking
- Attack sequence: Ram then Bite
- AT 1 (fur)

## Trolls (NPC/Monster)

**Note:** NPC Trolls from Creature Law are NOT the same as PC Trolls from Core Law. PC Trolls use the Core Law racial entry. If you need NPC troll opponents, reference Creature Law for the appropriate stat block.

## General Creature Combat Notes

- Creatures attack using natural weapon tables (Bite, Claw, Ram, etc.)
- Size matters: Large creatures deal +1 or more critical severity
- Many creatures have attack sequences (e.g., Ram followed by Bite)
- Check Creature Law for specific resistances, vulnerabilities, and special abilities
- Fear/morale: intelligent creatures may flee when badly hurt; animals may flee when pack leader falls
