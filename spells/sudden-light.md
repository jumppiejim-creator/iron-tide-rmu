## Effect

Causes a 10-foot radius burst of intense light centered on the target point. All creatures within the area of effect must make an Essence Resistance Roll or be stunned.

## Stun Mechanics

- **Stun duration:** 1 round per 10 failure on the Essence RR
- **Stun penalty:** -50 to all actions while stunned
- **RR calculation:** Target rolls d100 open-ended + Essence RR bonus. Must meet or exceed the caster's SCR result.
- **Failure margin:** Caster's SCR minus target's RR = failure amount. Divide by 10 (round down) = rounds of stun.

### Example
Rian casts Sudden Light with SCR result of 98. A soldier with Essence RR +6 rolls 41. Total RR = 47. Failure margin = 98 - 47 = 51. Stun duration = 5 rounds at -50.

## Tactical Notes

- **AoE stun is the party's best opener.** A single Sudden Light can neutralize multiple enemies for several rounds, allowing the party to engage on their terms.
- **Friendly fire:** Allies within the 10' radius must also resist. Position accordingly.
- **Line of sight:** Rian needs to see the target point. Darkness, walls, and total cover block it.
- **Repeated use:** Each casting costs 5 PP. At 141 PP, Rian can cast this approximately 28 times before running dry.
- **Nightvision does not protect against Sudden Light.** It is a magical burst, not ambient light. Nightvision creatures are affected normally.
- **Enemies with high Essence RR** (other spellcasters, magical creatures, elves) will resist more often. Against mundane soldiers with low RR, it's devastating.
- **Does no damage.** It only stuns. Follow up with attacks from the party to capitalize on the stun window.

## Interaction with Other Spells

- **Darkness I:** Sudden Light can be cast into a Darkness zone. The flash occurs and dissipates — it does not dispel the Darkness.
- **Resist Light:** Creatures under Resist Light effects may gain bonuses to their RR at GM discretion.
