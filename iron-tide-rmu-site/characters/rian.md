## KEY SPELL COMBOS

### Opening Salvo
1. **Sudden Light** (Light Law 5, 2 AP via Quick-Caster)  10'R AoE stun at 100' range. Stuns everyone in the area for 1 round per 10 failure. Sets up the whole party.
2. Follow with Shock Bolt or reposition with remaining 2 AP.

### Sustained Damage
- **Shock Bolt I** (2 AP)  quick lightning bolt every round at 25' range.
- **Ice Bolt I** (3+ AP)  cold bolt for variety vs lightning-resistant foes.
- **Fire Bolt I** (3+ AP)  heat bolt for additional elemental coverage.

### Party Protection
- **Resist Cold Sphere** (Elemental Shields 6)  10'R cold protection before dropping Ice effects near Grond.
- **Resist Light** (Elemental Shields 1)  another option for Grond's light sensitivity, +10 RR/DB vs electricity.
- **Protection II** (Spell Wall 5)  +10 to target's RR vs all spells.

### Battlefield Control
- **Wall of Fire** (Fire Law 4)  block an approach route.
- **Wall of Cold** (Ice Law 3)  another blocking option.
- **Darkness I** (Light Law 6)  create darkness zones. Party members with Nightvision fight normally; enemies without it are blind.

## TACTICAL NOTES

- **Glass cannon:** 40 hits in AT 1 means a single solid hit could drop this character. Stay behind Grond, stay at range, stay alive.
- **Quick-Caster II:** Lightning spells at 2 AP is the defining ability. Cast Shock Bolt and still move, or cast Sudden Light and reposition. Two casts per round is theoretically possible at 2 AP each.
- **Sudden Light + Party:** Stun a group, then Grond wades in with Krush criticals and Ambrosia flanks for Ambush strikes. The signature Iron Tide combo.
- **Elemental Shields before Ice near allies:** Always cast Resist Cold Sphere before dropping Cold effects in areas where Grond is fighting. Grond has Cold Resistance III (+15) already, but the Sphere adds another +10.
- **Darkness combo:** Cast Darkness I on a group of enemies. The Gnome, Warden, Ambrosia, and Grond all have Nightvision  they fight normally in the dark. Enemies without it are effectively blind.
- **Spell versatility:** Access to all six elemental lists plus two Open Essence lists means there's always an answer. Lightning-resistant? Switch to Cold. Cold-resistant? Switch to Fire. Magic-heavy enemies? Spell Wall.
- **70 PP pool:** This is massive for level 3. Sustainable casting across multiple encounters per day.

# PARTY TACTICS REFERENCE

## Formation

**Front Line:** Grond (center, wall shield covering flanks)

**Flankers:** Ambrosia (mobile, repositioning for flank/rear attacks)

**Back Line:** The Warden (ranged bow fire, buffs), The Stormcaller (elemental bolts, area denial)

## Key Synergies

- **Ralhann  Party:** Wolf Senses and Cat Eyes give unmatched scouting capability. Rabbit Reflexes makes him the fastest combatant in any fight.
- **Stormcaller  Everyone:** Sudden Light (AoE stun) sets up the entire party  Grond hammers stunned foes, Ambrosia flanks for Ambush crits
- **Stormcaller  Grond:** Resist Cold Sphere before dropping Cold effects near Grond. Darkness I blinds enemies while all four party members have Nightvision
- **Grond  Everyone:** Wall shield + Protect skill lets him extend shield DB to adjacent allies
- **Ambrosia + Grond:** Grond holds enemies in place, Ambrosia flanks for Ambush-enhanced criticals
- **Grond's Riposte:** Enemies attacking Grond face a lose-lose: either they fail to hit (and eat a counter-attack) or they ignore him (and Ambrosia stabs them from behind)
- **Initiative Order:** Warden (+11)  Stormcaller (+10)  Ambrosia (+9)  Grond (+4). Casters buff and debuff first, then martials capitalize.

## The Iron Tide Signature Combo

1. **Stormcaller** opens with Sudden Light (2 AP, Quick-Caster)  AoE stun in 10'R
2. **Warden** fires composite bow into stunned targets (+74 OB)
3. **Ambrosia** closes to flank stunned enemies  Ambush +48 enhances critical rolls
4. **Grond** wades in with warhammer  Big Krush criticals at +80 OB against stunned, flanked foes

## Combat Round Checklist

1. **Initiative Phase:** Roll 2d10 + Qu bonus for each combatant
2. **Action Phases (4 per round):** Each combatant spends AP per phase
3. **Declare parry** when attacked (OB  DB transfer)
4. **Resolve attacks** when enough AP spent (4 AP full, 3 AP at -25, 2 AP at -50)
5. **Upkeep Phase:** Subtract bleeding, reduce spell/stun durations

## Party Weaknesses

- **Essence Magic:** Grond has -13 Essence RR. Enemy Magicians are dangerous to him.
- **Fear:** Grond (+3) and Warden (+5) have low Fear RR. Undead and fear-causing creatures are a concern.
- **Light:** Grond's Light Sensitivity I means -25 in bright sunlight. Mitigated by buying down from Tier II, but direct sunlight still hurts. Favor night raids, cave clearing, and cloudy-day operations.
- **Hits Disparity:** The Stormcaller has 40 hits, the Warden 64. Both are fragile. One solid hit past their DB could be devastating.
- **AoE Friendly Fire:** The Stormcaller's ball spells and Sudden Light hit everyone in the area. Elemental Shields and careful targeting are essential.
