GameMaster LawGameMaster Law
Character Law provides the roles to be
played as well as the skills and the maneuvers
to use them in. Arms Law offers
the characters the challenge of battle, with
its risks and dangers. Here, Gamemaster
Law provides the remaining tools a gamemaster
will need to create their stories and
adventures.
Chapter 12 provides the tools for designing
a campaign, defining the tone and
power level of the game, and customizing
the races, cultures, and professions to suit
the setting. Guidelines are provided for experience
points, to reward players for accomplishing
tasks and advancing the story.
Chapter 13 covers health, healing, and
death, including the treatment of injuries
and their natural recovery.
In Chapter 14, the psychology and interactions
of non-player characters are described,
providing guidance in determining
their reactions. Players may need to persuade,
inspire, or deceive them to achieve
their goals, depending on whether they are
allies, adversaries, or simply inhabitants of
the world the gamemaster is creating.
The final chapter describes hazards of the
environment. These include extremes of
temperature, drowning, falling, poison,
disease, and darkness.
Being the Gamemaster of a roleplaying
game involves a lot of separate jobs rolled
up into one. The GM must direct the action,
act out all the non-player roles, resolve
disputes, and keep the flow of the game
moving. Stepping back from the action,
the GM also writes all the elements of the
story not controlled by the players. Rolemaster
can be used as the basis for many
different stories, and the rules in these sections
will help the GM get the job done at
the gaming table and customize the game
to fit the style of action he wants to run.
