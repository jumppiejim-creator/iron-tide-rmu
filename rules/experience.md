# Experience Points Rules Reference

## Awarding Experience

Characters gain Experience Points (EP) by participating in significant events. EP is awarded by the GM at the end of each session. A character's level equals EP divided by 10,000, rounded down. Level 1 begins at 10,000 EP.

Target pacing: approximately 1 level every 2-3 sessions.

---

## Event Types

If more than one event type applies, award both. A character who personally accomplishes something that also moves the session forward gets both the personal EP and the session EP. The whole party receives session and story EP equally (not split).

---

## Personal Events

Individual accomplishments that fulfill a character's personal plans or desires.

| Category | EP Range | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Minor | 10-100 | Achieving a desire without a specific plan. Stumbling into something personally meaningful. |
| Moderate | 100-500 | Actively working toward a goal when it is achieved. The character planned for this. |
| Major | 500-1000 | Long-range planning and sustained effort to achieve something personally significant. |

**Examples:**
- Minor: A character who values reputation witnesses enemies flee at the sight of them.
- Moderate: A character who planned an ambush executes it perfectly, using specific skills toward a tactical goal.
- Major: A character who has spent multiple sessions cultivating an asset finally turns a critical NPC.

**Failure:** If the player roleplays disappointment and learns from a poor result, the GM may award EP for the failure as a learning experience.

---

## Session Events

Achievements during play that moved the session forward. These are usually shared by the whole party. Each session should include at least one, but not more than a handful.

| Category | EP Range | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Minor | 100-500 | Any accomplishment that contributes significantly to the session's success. |
| Moderate | 500-1000 | Something where failure would have made the session's major objective far more difficult or impossible. |
| Major | 1000-5000 | The entire point of the session. Did the characters accomplish what they set out to do? |

**Examples:**
- Minor: Preventing an alarm from sounding during a raid. Successful stealth approach.
- Moderate: Securing critical intelligence that enables the next phase of the campaign. Turning a key NPC.
- Major: Capturing an enemy stronghold. Winning the climactic battle. Completing the mission.

**Failure:** If the failure advances the story and the players tried their hardest despite bad odds, the GM may still award session event EP. An epic defense before a forced retreat should not be punished.

---

## Story Events

Major, long-term events that advance the campaign's overarching plot. Almost always shared by the whole party. Most sessions will have at most one minor story event. Moderate and major story events are rare — they represent climactic moments.

| Category | EP Range | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Minor | 10-100 | Some forward movement in the overall campaign. |
| Moderate | 100-500 | Significant campaign progress. A meaningful shift in the strategic situation. |
| Major | 500-1000 | A turning point. The climax of many sessions of effort. Drastically shifts the campaign. |

**Examples:**
- Minor: Gaining a new piece of intelligence about the enemy's operations.
- Moderate: Disrupting a major supply chain. Establishing a forward base. Identifying a key enemy agent.
- Major: Destroying the enemy's command structure. Completing a long-running objective that changes the balance of power.

---

## Slower or Faster Advancement

- **Slower leveling:** Halve all EP awards. Characters level every 4-6 sessions.
- **Faster leveling:** Double all EP awards. Characters level every 1-2 sessions. Never gain more than 1 level at once.
- **Graduated pacing:** Faster leveling for levels 1-5, normal for 6-20, slower for 21+.

---

## Development Points as Experience (Optional)

Instead of accumulating EP toward level thresholds, the GM may award Development Points (DP) directly. Roughly 150 EP = 1 DP. Characters use DP immediately but must still follow leveling restrictions (e.g., rapid development costs). A character is considered to have gained a level after being awarded 60 DP.

| Event Type | DP Range |
|------------|----------|
| Personal | 1-6 DP |
| Session | 1-30 DP |
| Story | 3-60 DP |
