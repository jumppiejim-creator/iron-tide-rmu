# Social and Infiltration Maneuvers Reference

## Overview

Social interactions in RMU use the same Static Maneuver system as other non-combat actions: d100 open-ended + skill bonus + difficulty modifier. The GM sets difficulty based on the NPC's disposition, the situation, and how reasonable the request is.

---

## Social Skills

### Charm
Persuasion, winning people over, making a favorable impression. Used when the character is being honest (or at least sincere-seeming) about their intentions. Covers: requesting favors, negotiating cooperation, calming hostility, inspiring confidence, making friends.

### Duping
Lying, deceiving, misleading. Used when the character is deliberately misrepresenting the truth. Covers: passing off a false identity, lying about intentions, planting misinformation, feigning emotions, bluffing.

### Social Awareness
Reading people, detecting lies, assessing mood and motivation. Primarily a reactive/passive skill. Covers: detecting deception (opposed by Duping), reading emotional state, gauging how well a negotiation is going, noticing hidden agendas.

### Trading
Negotiation and deal-making. Used when there's a transaction or bargain involved. Covers: haggling prices, negotiating contracts, finding buyers/sellers, assessing fair value, brokering agreements.

### Trickery
Sleight of hand, misdirection, physical deception. Not verbal lying (that's Duping) — this is about what the hands and body do. Covers: pickpocketing, planting objects, card tricks, stage magic, concealing items on person.

## Difficulty Guidelines for Social Maneuvers

| Difficulty | Mod | When to Use |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Routine (+30) | +30 | NPC is already inclined to agree. Asking a friend for a small favor. |
| Easy (+20) | +20 | NPC is neutral and the request is reasonable. Normal merchant negotiation. |
| Light (+10) | +10 | NPC is slightly reluctant but persuadable. Asking for a discount. |
| Medium (+0) | +0 | NPC needs convincing. Turning a neutral party into a cooperator. Standard difficulty. |
| Hard (-10) | -10 | NPC is opposed but not hostile. Convincing a guard to look the other way. |
| Very Hard (-20) | -20 | NPC is hostile or the request is unreasonable. Talking down an enemy. |
| Ext. Hard (-30) | -30 | NPC is deeply opposed. Turning an enemy asset mid-conversation. |
| Sheer Folly (-50) | -50 | NPC has every reason to refuse. Convincing a loyal bodyguard to betray their lord on the spot. |

## Circumstance Modifiers

These stack with difficulty. The GM should apply what makes sense.

| Circumstance | Modifier |
|-------------|----------|
| NPC is afraid of the party | +10 to +30 |
| Party has leverage (evidence, threats, debts) | +10 to +20 |
| NPC stands to benefit from cooperation | +10 to +20 |
| Character has established rapport over time | +5 to +15 |
| NPC has been drinking or is distracted | +5 to +10 |
| Character's request aligns with NPC's values | +5 to +15 |
| NPC has reason to distrust the character | -10 to -20 |
| Character is an outsider / unknown | -5 to -10 |
| NPC is being watched by allies/superiors | -10 to -20 |
| Request puts NPC at personal risk | -10 to -30 |
| Cultural or language barrier | -5 to -15 |
| Character's appearance is threatening or inappropriate | -5 to -15 |

## Opposed Social Rolls

When one character is actively trying to deceive another, use opposed rolls:

**Duping vs Social Awareness:** The deceiver rolls d100 OE + Duping. The target rolls d100 OE + Social Awareness. Compare results — higher wins. If the deceiver wins, the lie is believed. If the target wins, they detect the deception (though they may not know what the truth is).

**Charm vs Resistance:** When attempting to influence someone against their interests, the GM may call for the NPC to resist with a d100 OE + SD bonus (or Social Awareness) against the Charm result. This is appropriate for significant NPCs with strong motivations.

## Result Interpretation for Social Maneuvers

| Total | Result | Social Interpretation |
|-------|--------|----------------------|
| ≤ 0 | Absolute Failure | NPC is offended or alarmed. Relationship damaged. May trigger hostility. |
| 01-05 | Spectacular Failure | NPC sees through the attempt completely. Trust lost. |
| 06-25 | Failure | NPC is unconvinced. May try again later at -10 (they're wary now). |
| 26-75 | Partial Success | NPC is partially convinced. They might cooperate with conditions, or give partial information. |
| 76-110 | Near Success | NPC is mostly convinced but wants something in return, or needs one more push. |
| 111-175 | Success | NPC is convinced. They cooperate, share information, or agree to the deal. |
| 176+ | Absolute Success | NPC is fully won over. Exceeds expectations — may volunteer extra information or ongoing cooperation. |

## Extended Social Encounters

For complex social situations (infiltrating a party, running a con, negotiating a treaty), break the encounter into stages. Each stage requires a separate maneuver. Success at earlier stages can provide bonuses to later ones (+5 to +10 per prior success). Failure at a stage may increase difficulty of subsequent stages or close off options entirely.

**Example — Infiltrating a merchant's warehouse:**
1. **Charm the door guard** (Medium +0) — get inside the building
2. **Duping the clerk** (Hard -10) — pretend to be an expected visitor
3. **Social Awareness** (Medium +0) — figure out which office has the records
4. **Trickery** (Hard -10) — lift the key from a hook without being noticed

## Disguise and False Identity

Maintaining a false identity over time requires periodic Duping checks:
- **Initial establishment:** One Duping check against the most perceptive observer
- **Casual interaction:** No check needed if the disguise is plausible
- **Pointed questions:** Duping check at the difficulty of the question
- **Sustained scrutiny:** Duping check every scene, with cumulative -5 per check after the first

Physical disguise (costumes, makeup) uses Trickery for the setup. The quality of the disguise provides a modifier to subsequent Duping checks (+5 for adequate, +10 for good, +20 for excellent).

Props and credentials (like a Trelaine authorization token or signet ring) provide +10 to +20 to relevant social checks, depending on how recognizable and authoritative they are.
